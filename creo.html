Creo Apparel Case Study

Creo Apparel is an online store, that helps everyone find products quickly and easily to match their particular needs.

Objective

The objective of this project was to use the user stories and brief, supplied by my UI Design course at CareerFoundry, to design an online store, that helps everyone find products quickly and easily to match their particular needs.

The Approach

In this project I was very excited as I was working on something existing and mine. My own streetwear brand named Creo Apparel that I created a year ago and I sell clothes with my own designs only through instagram. So I thought I would take advantage of this project to create a responsive web app so I can sell my products easier and faster.

User Stories

  • “As a customer, I want to be able to place multiple items in a shopping cart, so that I can purchase more than one item at a time.”
  • “As a customer, I want to be able to see when stock is on sale, so that I can save money.”
  • “As a customer, I want to have access to advanced filtering options, so that I don't have to browse through a lot of products to find what I'm looking for.”

Problem

One of the main problems that online store users face is that they are often forced to create a profile or log in and this is annoying because it wastes their time. Also after a short research I did I noticed that online stores that sell many different products often make it complicated to navigate their application, especially the filter section. So users sometimes get confused and may not find what they are looking for.

Solution

For this reason, Creo Apparel users do not need to create an account to navigate the app. They will only need to provide some information when it's time to order a product and complete their order. Furthermore, the actions the user has to take to find the product they want are quite few, as well as the filters menu is quite simple and easy to understand.

What could have gone better

Although I covered the project brief and user stories with the screens I designed, I could have gone deeper into the checkout screen and created more screens to show more detail (Billing Details, Shipping Method, Add a card).

What went well

As I said above I believe that I followed the brief and I have met the required objectives as in the web app I created users can have quick and easy access to their cart in order to add and remove products. Also, the products that are on sale are quite visible and the filter structure is pretty simple and detailed at the same time just as users would like.

Future Iterations

As for the future, this project will soon come to life as it's time to have my own web app and sell my products from there. Once I create all the screens and do some refinements, it will be ready to go into the final phase of development.
